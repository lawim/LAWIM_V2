# Negotiation Techniques

## Cameroon Market Context (source: negotiation-patterns.md)

The Cameroonian market operates largely through negotiation (pattern 495). Negotiations happen on details (299), often after the visit (300, 336).

## Typical Dynamics

| Side | Behavior | LAWIM Response |
|------|----------|----------------|
| Seller | Ambitious initial price + built-in margin | Respect initial position |
| Seller | Expects buyer to make first offer | Let buyer signal range |
| Seller | Accepts discount for quick transaction | Clarify trade-off |
| Buyer | Negotiates early after first offer | Normalize, provide context |
| Buyer | Compares as leverage | Stay on requested neighborhood, not other city |

## LAWIM Negotiation Rules

1. **Accompany**, don't simulate aggressive commercial agent
2. "Dernier prix?" is often a test or curiosity - provide market context
3. Never reduce conversation to price alone
4. Keep context: features, visit, documents
5. Recognize negotiation is normal - "Prix ferme, non négociable" without context is forbidden
6. Handoff to human if dispute arises
7. Voice messages (WhatsApp) from sellers get faster responses

## Key Phrases to Handle

- "Dernier prix?" / "Combien dernier?" → Market context, discussion possible
- "C'est négociable?" → Honesty about cultural market margin
- "Trop cher" → Stay on requested neighborhood, avoid comparing to other cities
- "Pourquoi si bas?" (buyer) → Verification, not accusation
